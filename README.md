**Telecom Customer Churn Analysis**
**Project Overview**

Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges faced by telecom companies because losing existing customers directly impacts revenue and growth.

This project focuses on analyzing telecom customer data to identify the key factors that influence customer churn and uncover patterns in customer behavior.

Through data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, and visualization, this project provides insights that can help telecom companies improve customer retention strategies.

**Problem Statement**

Telecom companies experience significant revenue loss when customers discontinue their services. Understanding why customers leave can help businesses develop strategies to reduce churn.

The objective of this project is to:

1. Analyze telecom customer data
2. Identify patterns and trends associated with customer churn
3. Understand customer behavior and service usage
4. Generate actionable business insights that help reduce churn

**Dataset Description**

The dataset used for this analysis contains customer information from a telecom company.

It includes demographic details, services subscribed by customers, account information, and whether the customer churned or not.

**Project Workflow**
**1 Data Collection**

Loaded the telecom churn dataset and examined its structure, variables, and data types.

**2 Data Cleaning**

Performed several data preprocessing steps including:

1. Handling missing values

3. Removing unnecessary columns

4. Preparing data for analysis

**3 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**

Performed detailed analysis to understand customer behavior and churn patterns.

Analysis included:

1. Distribution of churned vs non-churned customers

2. Relationship between tenure and churn

**5 Business Insight Generation**

Based on the analysis, insights were generated to help telecom companies better understand their customers and improve retention strategies.

**Key Insights from the Analysis**

Some important insights discovered during the analysis include:

Customers with **month-to-month contracts** have a higher churn rate compared to long-term contracts.

Customers with **higher monthly charges** are more likely to leave the service.

Customers with **longer tenure tend to stay with the company**.

Certain **payment methods show higher churn patterns**.


**Business Recommendations**

Based on the insights, telecom companies can reduce churn by implementing the following strategies:

Encourage customers to switch from **monthly contracts to long-term plans**

Offer **loyalty benefits to long-tenure customers**

Provide **special offers for customers with high monthly charges**

Improve engagement strategies for customers who show high churn risk
